# DiagramPilot Brand Use Policy

This policy explains how to use the DiagramPilot product identity. It is
separate from the MIT Code License. The MIT Code License grants copyright
permissions for the repository contents; it does not grant trademark rights,
endorsement rights, or permission to present unofficial work as an official
DiagramPilot release.

## Covered Brand Elements

The DiagramPilot brand includes:

- The DiagramPilot name.
- The DiagramPilot mark and any future official mark.
- The DiagramPilot wordmark and any future official wordmark.
- The `diagrampilot.com` domain and public website identity.
- Official package names, release names, release notes, version labels, and
  other release identity.

## Allowed Use

You may use the DiagramPilot brand elements to:

- Refer truthfully to DiagramPilot, its packages, its public docs, or compatible
  integrations.
- Link to `https://diagrampilot.com` and official repository materials.
- Identify that a project imports, wraps, extends, tests, or documents
  DiagramPilot.
- Use unmodified DiagramPilot marks in articles, talks, screenshots, or docs
  when the use is informational and does not imply endorsement.
- Name forks or derivative projects in a way that makes their unofficial status
  clear.

## Disallowed Use

You may not use the DiagramPilot brand elements to:

- Imply sponsorship, endorsement, employment, partnership, or official status
  without written permission.
- Publish modified packages, binaries, docs, websites, or release artifacts as
  official DiagramPilot releases.
- Use the DiagramPilot name, mark, wordmark, domain, or release identity as the
  primary branding for another product, service, package, organization, social
  account, or domain.
- Register names or domains that are confusingly similar to DiagramPilot or
  `diagrampilot.com`.
- Alter the DiagramPilot mark or wordmark in a way that makes unofficial work
  look official.

## Forks And Derivatives

Forks, patched builds, and derivative tools are welcome under the MIT Code
License, but they must use their own product identity. Make the relationship to
DiagramPilot clear with language such as "based on DiagramPilot" or "compatible
with DiagramPilot" instead of presenting the work as DiagramPilot itself.

## Questions

For brand use questions, open an issue in the DiagramPilot repository and
describe the intended use.
